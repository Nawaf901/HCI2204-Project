Ethics in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI 2204)

Department: Software Engineering | College: Computing

Credit Hours: 2 | Level: 2nd year/ 4th level

Course General Description

This course examines the ethical considerations that arise in human-computer interaction, focusing on moral principles, privacy, intellectual property, and social responsibilities. Students will analyze ethical dilemmas and frameworks, explore inclusivity and accessibility, and evaluate the societal impacts of HCI practices and emerging technologies.

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

Code Course Learning Outcomes Teaching Strategies Assessment Methods
1.1 Explain ethical, legal, and social frameworks relevant to HCI, including privacy and data protection. Discussions, case studies Assignments/ Quizzes/Exams
2.1 Communicate ethical analyses effectively through case studies and professional discussions. Discussions, demonstrations Assignments/ Research Project
3.1 Demonstrate informed judgment and uphold professional ethical principles in HCI practices. Case studies, discussions Research project / exams

Course Topics

  1. Introduction to Ethics in HCI
  2. Ethical Frameworks and Theories
  3. Privacy, Security, and Civil Liberties
  4. Intellectual Property and Software Ethics
  5. Social Implications of Computing
  6. Inclusivity, Accessibility, and Cultural Contexts
  7. Professional Responsibility in HCI
  8. Emerging Technologies and Ethical Challenges
  9. Case Studies in HCI Ethics
  10. Ethical Research and Experimentation
